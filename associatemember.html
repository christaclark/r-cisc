R-CISC Membership

Associate Members are industry leaders that are committed to the R-CISC mission and support to further the R-CISC mission by becoming an associate member. An Associate Member, can visibly demonstrate their industry leadership and commitment to retail cyber intelligence and data security through membership in R-CISC. There are tiered levels of membership within the R-CISC offered at either the Platinum or Premiere Tiers.

Joining the R-CISC is an important leap in gaining access to actionable intelligence while making the industry stronger and safer against the onslaught of threats. Being involved with R-CISC through our events, Associate programs or other activities supports the industry and helps to position your company and its leaders as a thought leader and trusted advisor in the Retail and Commercial Services ecosystem. By joining the R-CISC Associate program, it is expected that all Associates will and do agree to support the R-CISC by securely and freely sharing information, promoting RCS-ISAC events & workshops, providing value-driven referrals and introductions to future potential members of R-CISC – and to help grow its membership base, which grows the value for all involved and supports the Retail industry with and increased focus on actionable member benefit against threat and risks daily.

If you would like to be emailed a membership application or learn more information, please email us at membership@r-cisc.org.

Membership Qualifications:

In order to qualify for Associate membership in R-CISC, the applicant must meet the following requirements:

1. Entity must be engaged in supporting retailers or commercial services sectors in cyber security threat intelligence, research, or product delivery.

2. Entity must not be wholly or partially state-owned; must be privately owned or publicly owned at the ultimate parent company level without any ownership or control, directly or indirectly, by any instrumentality of any government.

3. Must not have its primary business headquarters in a country that is subject to U.S. economic sanctions as indicated by the U.S. Department of the Treasury’s Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), or itself be identified or designated as sanctioned by OFAC and must not have its head office or have its primary business in a country that does not have laws targeting cybercrime or does not actively prosecute cyber-criminals in that country;

4. Must not have its head office or its primary business in a country that supports terrorist activities or corporate espionage against the United States.
